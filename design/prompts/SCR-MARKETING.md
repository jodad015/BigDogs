# SCR-MARKETING — Design Prompt

## Form Factor
iPhone 390x844 for mobile layout. Also design a desktop variant at 1440px wide.

## Design Direction
The storefront. Dark, bold, high-energy. This page sells the competition —
not the health benefits. Think sports promo, not wellness app. The logo and
brand navy dominate. Crimson accent pops on CTAs. Every section should feel
like it's daring you to step up.

Dark background throughout — no light sections. This is the brand at full volume.

## Variants

Create **2 variants**:

---

### VARIANT 1 — "SCR-MARKETING / Mobile"

Single column, scrolling page.

**Hero section:**
- Full-bleed brand navy background
- Logo (dog + barbell) centered, large — this is the first thing you see.
  White/cream version on dark background. Sized ~150px wide.
- "BIGDOGS" in large bold uppercase below logo, white, heavy tracking
- Tagline: "Prove it on the scale." — muted cream, medium text
- CTA button: "Get Started" — crimson accent, full width, bold white text,
  rounded, prominent. Links to app.bigdogs.app
- Subtle downward scroll indicator (chevron or line)

**What is BigDogs? section:**
- Slightly different dark background shade for visual separation (#0f0f1a vs #1a1a2e)
- Section headline: "A Weight Loss Competition" — white, bold, left-aligned
- Short paragraph: "Challenge your friends. Track your progress. Win with
  consistency — not crash diets. BigDogs rewards hitting your pace, not
  racing to the bottom." — muted cream text, readable line length
- Optional: simple illustration or icon of a scale/trophy

**How It Works section:**
- Back to navy background
- Section headline: "How It Works" — white, bold
- 4 steps in a vertical stack (mobile), each with:
  - Step number in accent color circle (1, 2, 3, 4)
  - Bold white label
  - Muted description text
- Steps:
  1. "Set Your Pace" — "Pick a goal. The app computes your weekly target."
  2. "Weigh In Daily" — "Step on the scale each morning. Your 7-day trend does the rest."
  3. "Compete Weekly" — "Every Friday, scores drop. Stay on pace to climb the board."
  4. "Win the Challenge" — "Most placement points at the end takes the crown."

**Features section:**
- Darker background shade
- Section headline: "Built for Competition"
- Feature cards in a vertical stack, each with:
  - Icon (small, accent or muted)
  - Bold label
  - One-line description
- Features:
  - "Trend Tracking" — "7-day rolling average smooths the noise"
  - "Showdown Weeks" — "Last Friday of the month. Double points."
  - "Solo Mode" — "Track your weight without a challenge"
  - "Public Leaderboard" — "Let spectators follow along — no weight numbers exposed"
  - "Safety Rails" — "Smart guardrails keep goals healthy and sustainable"

**Footer CTA section:**
- Navy background
- "Ready to step up?" — large bold white text, centered
- "Get Started" button — same crimson accent style as hero
- Below: small muted text "Free to use. No ads. No nonsense."

### VARIANT 2 — "SCR-MARKETING / Desktop (1440px)"

Same content as mobile, adapted for wide viewport.

**Hero:**
- Logo and text centered, more generous vertical spacing
- CTA button constrained width (~300px), centered

**What is BigDogs? + How It Works:**
- Content constrained to ~800px centered column
- How It Works steps in a 2x2 grid instead of vertical stack

**Features:**
- Feature cards in a 3-column grid (or 2+3 layout)
- Cards have subtle dark border or elevated background

**Footer CTA:**
- Same centered layout, generous padding

**General:**
- Max content width ~1200px, centered
- More whitespace (dark-space) between sections
- Typography scales up slightly for desktop
